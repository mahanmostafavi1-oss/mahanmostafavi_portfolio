About

Background

A little on who I am, where I've been, and what I'm working toward.

I'm a dual Biology and Economics student at Lehigh University, graduating in May 2027, with a minor in Business. I grew up on Long Island, attended Portledge School where I completed the IB program, and spent a summer studying abroad in Milan — an experience that sharpened my interest in how economies and institutions shape the way people live.

My academic path has moved between the sciences and finance. I spent time as a Visiting Research Scholar at the Feinstein Institutes, working on IRF5 and bone remodeling using mouse models, PCR, and isothermal assembly. That work taught me rigor and patience. At the same time, I've been drawn toward investment banking and the analytical side of capital markets, and I'm actively pursuing that direction post-graduation.

Outside the classroom, I co-founded the Diane Fund, raising over $150K and coordinating 50+ volunteers. I serve as Vice President of the Lehigh Chess Club. I was a member of TAMID from 2022 to 2025, contributing to projects including the Tutterra AI tutoring platform. I also served as DEI Chair of Chi Phi.

I'm interested in the intersection of data, decision-making, and institutions — whether that's in finance, development economics, or the frameworks that shape how people and organizations allocate resources.
